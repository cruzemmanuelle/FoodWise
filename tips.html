FOOD CONSERVATION TIPS

Plan Before You Shop

Make a grocery list and buy only what you need.

Planning ahead helps reduce impulse buying and food waste. Before heading to the store, check what you already have at home, create a detailed shopping list, and stick to it. Consider planning meals for the week so you only buy ingredients you’ll actually use. This saves money, time, and prevents unused food from going bad.

• Check your fridge and pantry first

• Make a weekly meal plan

• Write and stick to a shopping list

• Avoid shopping when hungry

• Buy only what you need

Store Food Properly

Keep food in airtight containers and refrigerate items that spoil quickly.

Proper storage keeps food fresh for longer and prevents spoilage. Use airtight containers to maintain freshness, refrigerate or freeze perishable items promptly, and store fruits and vegetables according to their needs. Keeping your fridge organized and at the right temperature also helps extend shelf life.

• Use airtight containers

• Keep fridge at the right temperature

• Store raw and cooked food separately

• Keep fruits and vegetables in proper sections

• Label stored food with dates

Serve Small Portions First

Take only what you can finish to avoid leftovers.

Start with smaller portions when serving meals to avoid unnecessary leftovers. You can always go back for more if you’re still hungry. This simple habit helps reduce plate waste and ensures that food isn’t thrown away after meals.

• Start with smaller servings

• Go for seconds if needed

• Use smaller plates or bowls

• Avoid overfilling plates

• Save untouched extras immediately

Check Expiration Dates

Use older items first before opening new ones.

Regularly check expiration and “best before” dates on your food items. Use older products first before opening newer ones, following a “first in, first out” system. This prevents food from being forgotten and expiring in the back of your fridge or pantry.

• Follow “first in, first out” rule

• Check dates weekly

• Keep older items in front

• Don’t open new items before finishing old ones

• Be aware of “best before” vs “expiry”

Reuse Leftovers

Turn leftover food into new meals instead of throwing it away.

Get creative with leftovers by turning them into new meals. For example, roasted vegetables can be added to soups or wraps, and cooked meats can be used in salads or sandwiches. This not only reduces waste but also saves time on cooking future meals.

• Turn leftovers into new meals

• Store leftovers safely

• Use within a few days

• Mix into soups, salads, or wraps

• Reheat properly before eating

Freeze Extra Food

Preserve excess food by freezing it for later use.

If you have excess food that you won’t use in time, freezing is a great option. Many foods like bread, cooked meals, fruits, and vegetables freeze well and can be used later. Label items with dates so you know when to use them.

• Freeze unused portions quickly

• Use freezer-safe bags or containers

• Label with date and name

• Portion food before freezing

• Defrost safely before use

Organize Your Pantry

Keep food visible and arranged to prevent forgetting items.

Keep your pantry neat and organized so you can easily see what you have. Arrange items by category and place older products at the front. A well-organized pantry helps prevent overbuying and ensures food doesn’t get forgotten and wasted.

• Group similar items together

• Keep frequently used items visible

• Rotate older items to the front

• Clean regularly

• Avoid overstocking
